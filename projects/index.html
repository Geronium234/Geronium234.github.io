Detective in Distress

Unity 2D Visual Novel C#

Overview

Detective in Distress is a 2D visual novel where players take on the role of a detective trying to solve a missing person case by travelling to different times.

My Role

I was responsible for three main areas of development:

  • Story slider system — programmed the sliding system using 3 diffrent images to create the illusion of scrolling through the story while using an arary to determin the previous, current, and next image to create a seamless loop of story progression.
  • 2D platforming — built the movement and collision systems used in the platforming segments that act as transitions between story beats.
  • Art integration — imported, sliced, and animated all 2D art assets in Unity, including character sprites, backgrounds, and UI elements.

Challenges & Solutions

One of the biggest challenges in this project was expanding my own knoladge of what I could do. I had a lot of ideas for this project that I didnt know how to implement and I was worried that I wouldnt be able to bring my vision to life. However, I found that with a bit of research and a lot of trial and error, I was able to make our vision of the game come to life and it felt great.

What I Learned

I learned how it felt to actually work as a team. I had worked on group projects before but this was the first time that I had sat down in a room with my teammates for hours and talked about diffrent ideas. Seeing little pieces of everyones perspective come together to create the end result felt sureal and I cant wait to work on more projects with other people in the future.

Kaboom

Godot 3D FPS GDScript

Overview

A personal project my friend and I built in Godot. Kaboom is a 3D FPS built around the idea of combining different bullets from other weapons. We made an intro level to showcase the mechanic.

My Role

I was responsible for programming the core gameplay mechanics:

  • Animation system — rigged and implemented first-person weapon animations including idle, fire, and reload states using Godot's AnimationPlayer and AnimationTree.
  • Shooting system — designed and programmed the bullet-combination mechanic, including projectile types, inventory management, and the logic for merging bullet properties when combined.
  • Level design — I created the layout for the level intro in godot and created the art assets for the level in blender.

The Bullet Combination Mechanic

We created the bullet combination mechanic by having the player have the weapon they wanted the bullet from equipped and then pressing the f button to select the weapon they wanted the bullet from. Then they swapped to the weapon they wanted the bullet in and pressed the f button to fire the combine the bullet to that weapon. Then when the user fires the weapon, the combined bullet is spawned.

Skyrise Frictionless Ascent

Unity 2D Vertical Platformer

Overview

Skyrise Frictionless Ascent is a vertical platformer where players control a character with no friction, making movement and momentum the key to navigating the level. I was responsible for creating everything in this project.

My Role

Solo project — I handled every aspect of development:

  • Physics-based controls — programmed the frictionless movement system, tuning momentum, gravity, and wall interactions to make the lack of friction feel intentional and fun rather than frustrating.
  • Level design — designed and built the vertical level layout, introducing new obstacles for the player.
  • Audio — sourced and implemented all sound effects and music, handling audio triggers and looping in Unity.
  • Animations — created and integrated all character and environment animations.

The Frictionless Movement System

The movement in Skyrise is built to be very restrictive and to an extent frustrating, but that is intentional. The player has a lot less control over the character than in a traditional platformer as they cant move while in the air and cant stop their momentum very easliy while on the ground. This creates a unique challenge for the player as they have to learn how to use the momentum to their advantage and to navigate the level rather than relying on precise control.

Challenges & Solutions

The biggest challenge by a long shot was creating the art for the game. I have never done pixel art before and it was very difficult as art isnt something I have a lot of experience with. It was a lot of trial and error but it was fun to learn a new skill and to see the game come together visually after creating the mechanics first.

What I Learned

I learned a lot about the full game development pipeline by doing this project solo. It significantly helped me when going into my group projects as I have a better understanding of how all the different aspects of game development come together and how to communicate with my teammates about different areas of development.
